Explore the fascinating letters and newsletters sent by Cosimo Bartoli (1503-1572), the Medici family’s trusted agent, during his decade-long stay in Venice from 1562 to 1572.

The letters, week after week, vividly capture the Venetian life, detailing the latest happenings and rumours
in Piazza San Marco and Rialto and shedding light on the Medici’s keen interests in the city.

The newsletters offer a unique glimpse into the circulation of information of the early modern period, revealing the richness
 and precision of the news that reached Venice, one of the most important informative hubs of the sixteenth century,
described by Cosimo I de’ Medici as the place where “all the news of the world converges."
